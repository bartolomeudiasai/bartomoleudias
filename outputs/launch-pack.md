## 1) Domain Name Recommendations

We need a name that is:
- credible
- flexible enough to expand beyond one PDF
- clearly connected to AI agents / autonomous work
- not too gimmicky
- usable as a brand, not just a campaign

## Recommended direction
The business should sound like:
- practical
- operator-led
- modern
- slightly premium
- not nerd cosplay

## Top domain recommendations

### 1. agentoperator.co.za
**Why it works:**
- strong positioning
- signals practical execution
- broad enough for products, services, and software
- memorable

### 2. buildyouragent.co.za
**Why it works:**
- clearer for the first product
- action-oriented
- easy to understand

### 3. practicalagent.co.za
**Why it works:**
- clean and direct
- good for a broad educational / product brand

### 4. hireanai.co.za
**Why it works:**
- strong marketing hook
- slightly bolder
- easy headline potential

### 5. agentstack.co.za
**Why it works:**

- broader long-term brand potential

- agentoperator.ai
- buildyouragent.ai
- hireanai.ai
- practicalagent.ai
- agentstack.ai

## Best pick
# **agentoperator.co.za**

Why I’d choose it:
- sounds like a real business
- not tied only to a PDF
- supports future offers: guides, templates, setups, consulting, tools
- feels more commercially serious than a descriptive course title

- the difference between a chatbot and an agent

- what changes once AI has memory, tools, and a job

### Chapter 2: What an AI Agent Actually Is
**Theme:** A practical mental model

Cover:
- role
- instructions
- memory
- tools
- triggers
- outputs
- approvals and boundaries

Explain the full stack in plain English.

### Chapter 3: Pick the Right First Job
**Theme:** Don’t build something stupid

Cover:
- what makes a good first use case
- recurring tasks
- low-risk, high-value workflows
- examples from work, life, and business
- how to decide what to automate first

### Chapter 4: Design the Agent
**Theme:** Give it a job description

Cover:
- identity and tone
- responsibilities
- allowed vs forbidden actions
- success criteria
- how to write a useful brief

### Chapter 5: Memory and Context
**Theme:** Why stateless AI is weak

Cover:
- what memory is for
- short-term vs long-term memory
- what to save
- what not to save
- how memory improves usefulness over time

### Chapter 6: Tools Make It Real
**Theme:** This is where the magic stops being fake

Cover:
- web search
- files
- email
- calendar
- messaging
- why tools matter more than prompt tricks

### Chapter 7: Guardrails and Trust
**Theme:** Don’t let your AI become an expensive idiot

Cover:
- privacy
- approvals
- trust ladder
- what should always require human confirmation
- how to scale confidence safely

### Chapter 8: Your First Workflow
**Theme:** Move from theory to action

Cover:
- pick one routine
- define the trigger
- define the output
- define the review point
- test it in the real world

### Chapter 9: Real Use Cases
**Theme:** Steal these ideas

Examples:
- research assistant
- prospecting assistant
- daily briefing agent
- family admin helper
- founder ops assistant
- side-hustle scout

### Chapter 10: South African Reality Check
**Theme:** Practical local framing

Cover:
- cost in rand
- tool selection
- privacy common sense
- business reality
- why this matters locally

### Chapter 11: Make It Actually Useful
**Theme:** Build routines, not demos

Cover:
- how to know something is working
- measuring usefulness
- keeping humans in the loop
- improving over time
- avoiding complexity addiction

### Chapter 12: What to Do Next
**Theme:** Turn one working setup into leverage

Cover:
- your next 7 days
- your next 30 days
- your next agent
- how to keep building capability
